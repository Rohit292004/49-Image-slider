NATURE
Sunsets are one of nature's most beautiful and inspiring phenomena. The vibrant colors of a sunset are due to a phenomenon called scattering. As the sun sets, its light has to pass through more of the Earth's atmosphere, scattering shorter blue and violet wavelengths and allowing the longer red and orange wavelengths to dominate the sky. The time of sunset varies throughout the year and is influenced by a location's latitude and longitude, as well as the time of year. During summer, sunsets are later in the evening, while in winter, they occur earlier.

PLANT
Leaves are essential organs of vascular plants, playing a critical role in photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert sunlight into energy. They come in a vast array of shapes, sizes, and colors, adapted to different environments and functions.

Mountain
Mountains are large landforms that rise prominently above their surroundings, typically characterized by steep slopes, significant height differences compared to surrounding terrain, and often rugged topography. They are formed through tectonic forces, volcanic activity, and erosion over millions of years.
